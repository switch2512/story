The Dark Forest
A Choose Your Own Adventure
Health: 100
Gold: 10
⁕ ⁕ ⁕
The Forest Edge
You stand at the edge of a vast, dark forest. The trees loom tall and twisted, their branches blocking out the moon. Somewhere deep within, you can hear the faint sound of running water — and something else. A low, rhythmic drumming.

Your village sent you here to find the missing merchant. You grip your lantern tightly.
You Wake Up
You push through tangled undergrowth, following the deep, steady beat. After an hour of walking, you emerge into a moonlit clearing. A circle of cloaked figures surrounds a bonfire, drumming in unison.

One of them turns and notices you. They pull back their hood — it's an old woman with kind eyes. "We wondered when a traveler would come," she says. "We know where the merchant is. But first — will you share our fire?"
The River
The sound of water leads you to a wide, dark river. The current is strong, but a rickety rope bridge spans the gap. On the far bank, you can just make out the faint glow of a lantern — and what looks like a cart with a broken wheel.

The merchant? The bridge creaks ominously in the wind. Downriver, a small rowboat is tied to a tree.
The Long Night
You find a sheltered hollow at the forest's edge and build a small fire. It's a wise decision — the forest at night is no place for the unprepared. As you rest, a fox trots up and sniffs at your pack.

Tied around its neck is a small piece of cloth — the same blue pattern as the missing merchant's coat. The fox watches you with unnervingly intelligent eyes.
The Circle of Fire
You sit by the fire. The old woman hands you a warm bowl of broth and tells you the merchant stumbled upon an old tomb to the north and went inside seeking treasure. He has not come out.

She presses a silver amulet into your hand. "The tomb is cursed. This will protect you — but only once." She points north. "Go before sunrise."
A Cold Reaction
The old woman's kind expression fades. The drumming stops. Every hooded figure turns to look at you in silence.

"Manners," she says quietly, "cost nothing." She turns her back on you. No one speaks. The figures resume their drumming as if you no longer exist.

You are alone in the dark forest again, no closer to finding the merchant.
Into the Dark
You back into the trees, heart pounding. But the forest has shifted — the path you came from is gone. The trees look different. Unfamiliar. The drumming fades behind you.

You walk for what feels like hours. Then — a light ahead. A lantern hanging from a post, and beneath it, a small wooden door set into the base of an enormous oak.
The Bridge
You step onto the rope bridge. It sways wildly. Halfway across, you hear a loud crack — one of the main ropes snaps. You lunge forward, scrambling hand over hand as the bridge collapses behind you.

You barely make it to the other side, scraping your hands on the rocks. But you're across. The lantern ahead flickers — and you hear a weak voice call out: "Hello? Is someone there?"
Downriver
The rowboat is old but sturdy. You row downriver for twenty minutes until you find a wide, shallow ford. You cross easily and double back along the far bank.

It takes longer, but you arrive safely at the cart. Beside it, wrapped in a blanket and nursing a twisted ankle, is the merchant. He looks up with enormous relief. "I thought no one was coming," he whispers.
No Answer
You call out. Your voice echoes across the water and into the dark trees beyond. The lantern on the far bank goes out.

Silence. Then — something large moves in the water beneath the bridge. A long shadow passes under the surface. You take a step back from the riverbank.
✦ Good Ending
The Merchant Found
With the merchant safe beside you, you navigate back through the forest as dawn begins to paint the sky gold. The trees seem less threatening in the light.

The village erupts in celebration when you return. The merchant, grateful beyond words, presses a heavy purse of coins into your hands. "You saved my life," he says simply.

That night, by the fire, you find yourself glancing toward the dark tree line — wondering what other secrets the forest holds.
✦ Bad Ending
Lost in the Dark
You run. The forest closes in around you. Every direction looks the same — twisted roots, dark bark, no stars above.

By morning, you are utterly lost. The merchant is never found. Travelers who pass the forest edge in the years that follow sometimes hear two voices calling out from the dark — both of them asking for help.
✦ Neutral Ending
An Incomplete Journey
You return to the village empty-handed, but alive. The merchant will be fine... probably.

You Wake Up
Your alarm is blaring. The faintest light can be seen through the thin curtains.

Shit. You're late for law school, you haven't read for class, and you're on call.
